Bicycling

Cycling works the same muscles as running

But with lower impact. If you’re going at an intense rate, you can burn anywhere from 600 to over a thousand calories a session, especially with use of intervals. Aim to keep your maximum heart rate about halfway between the low and high ends of your target heart rate zone.

Running

Miles to Smiles: Every stride shreds calories

Running is a fantastic calorie burner! It can incinerate between 280 and 520 calories in just 30 minutes, depending on your weight and intensity. While a general estimate suggests 100 calories per mile, remember this fluctuates based on factors like your body mass. The heavier you are, the more energy your body uses to move, resulting in greater calorie burn. So lace up your shoes, hit the ground, and torch some calories!”

Swimming

The lowest impact of all aerobic forms of exercise

Take the plunge and torch calories! Swimming is a low-impact calorie crusher, burning between 223 and 878 calories per hour depending on your stroke and intensity. From a leisurely backstroke to a powerful butterfly, every pool session gets your heart pumping and muscles working. So, dive in and make a splash towards your fitness goals!

Yoga

Unfold Your Power: Yoga burns & builds

Don't underestimate the power of yoga! This mind-body practice burns more than you might think. A one-hour yoga session can sizzle between 180 and 460 calories, depending on the style and your effort. From the flowing movements of Vinyasa to the restorative holds of Yin, yoga strengthens and tones while helping you torch calories. Unwind, refocus, and burn – all on your yoga mat!
